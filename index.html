👋 About Me

I’m Francis Boampong, a Ghanaian-American cybersecurity student based in Pueblo West, Colorado. Since relocating in 2020, I have completed an A.A.S. in Network Security and am pursuing a B.A. in Computer Science with a Cybersecurity emphasis at UCCS. My professional experience includes 30–40 hours per week supporting network operations and desktop environments at Amazon and as an IT work-study student at PCC.

Outside of school and work, I volunteer as a mentor for peer cybersecurity workshops and participate in National Cyber League competitions, where I apply real-world techniques to capture-the-flag scenarios.

🛡️ What I Do

I design and implement network defense strategies using tools like Wireshark and OpenVAS to identify and mitigate vulnerabilities. I automate routine tasks with Bash and Python scripts, improving system reliability and reducing manual configuration time by up to 40%.

In lab environments, I build virtual networks for penetration testing, develop custom security checklists, and document findings in detailed reports to guide remediation efforts.

I continuously research emerging threats, attend cybersecurity webinars, and contribute to open-source security projects to stay ahead of the latest attack vectors.
