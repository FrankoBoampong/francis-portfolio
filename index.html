👋 About Me

My name is Francis Boampong, a Ghanaian-American cybersecurity student based in Colorado Springs, Colorado. I earned an Associate of Applied Science in Network Security from Pueblo Community College and am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science with a specialization in Cybersecurity at the University of Colorado Colorado Springs.

My journey blends academic rigor with real-world application. While supporting IT operations at Pueblo Community College and Amazon, I developed technical fluency in network administration, vulnerability assessment, and secure system design. Outside formal education, I build independent cybersecurity projects, mentor peers, and participate in National Cyber League competitions to sharpen my skills. My focus is on defending digital environments with integrity, innovation, and precision.

🛡️ What I Do

I design and implement network defense strategies using tools like Wireshark and OpenVAS to identify and mitigate vulnerabilities. I automate routine tasks with Bash and Python scripts, improving system reliability and reducing manual configuration time by up to 40%.

In lab environments, I build virtual networks for penetration testing, develop custom security checklists, and document findings in detailed reports to guide remediation efforts.

I continuously research emerging threats, attend cybersecurity webinars, and contribute to open-source security projects to stay ahead of the latest attack vectors.
